# Grammar Rules — Complete Reference

> All **110 grammar rules** from the Czech-learning curriculum, grouped by unit.  
> Each entry: rule ID, name, pattern (one line), explanation (2–3 sentences), and first 2 CZ–EN example pairs.

---

## Unit 1 — Introduction to Czech Sounds & Writing

### GR-000 | First-syllable stress
- **Pattern:** Czech stress always falls on the first syllable of a word
- **Explanation:** In Czech, the stress always falls on the first syllable of every word. This is a fixed rule — unlike English, there are no exceptions. Long vowels are pronounced longer but do NOT receive extra stress.
- **Examples:**
  - *Praha* — "Prague" (stress on PRA)
  - *káva* — "coffee" (stress on KA)

### GR-001 | The letter ř
- **Pattern:** ř is a unique Czech sound — a rolled r with ž/š quality
- **Explanation:** The letter ř (r with háček) has no equivalent in other languages. It is a raised alveolar non-sonorant trill. To approximate: try saying 'r' and 'zh' simultaneously.
- **Examples:**
  - *řeka* — "river"
  - *tři* — "three"

### GR-002 | The letter ě and consonant softening
- **Pattern:** ě softens the preceding consonant: dě→ďe, tě→ťe, ně→ně, bě→bje, pě→pje, vě→vje, mě→mě
- **Explanation:** When ě follows certain consonants, it softens them. dě sounds like 'dye', tě like 'tye', ně like 'nye'. For bě, pě, vě: insert a 'j' sound (bje, pje, vje). mě is special — it sounds like 'mňe'.
- **Examples:**
  - *děti* — "children" (dě → ďe)
  - *těší* — "pleases" (tě → ťe)

### GR-003 | Long vs short vowels
- **Pattern:** á/é/í/ó/ú/ů are long versions of a/e/i/o/u — length changes meaning
- **Explanation:** Long vowels are marked with čárka (acute accent: á, é, í, ó, ú) or kroužek (ring: ů). A long vowel is pronounced approximately twice as long as a short one. Vowel length can change meaning.
- **Examples:**
  - *dům* — "house" (ů is long u)
  - *káva* — "coffee" (á is long a)

### GR-004 | Háček (ˇ) diacritic
- **Pattern:** č, š, ž, ř, ď, ť, ň — háček modifies consonant pronunciation
- **Explanation:** The háček (ˇ) mark changes the sound of consonants: č = ch (as in 'church'), š = sh (as in 'ship'), ž = zh (as in 'measure'), ř = unique Czech sound, ď = softened d, ť = softened t, ň = softened n (like Spanish ñ). These are distinct letters in the Czech alphabet, not modifications.
- **Examples:**
  - *čaj* — "tea"
  - *škola* — "school"

## Unit 2 — Greetings & Introductions

### GR-010 | Formal vs informal greeting
- **Pattern:** Use 'dobrý den' + 'Vy' for formal; 'ahoj' + 'ty' for informal
- **Explanation:** Czech has a T-V distinction. Use formal greetings (dobrý den, dobré ráno, dobrý večer) with strangers, elderly people, and in professional settings. Always use 'Vy' (formal you) with these. Using informal with someone who expects formal is considered rude.
- **Examples:**
  - *Dobrý den, jak se máte?* — "Good day, how are you? (formal)"
  - *Ahoj, jak se máš?* — "Hi, how are you? (informal)"

### GR-011 | Self-introduction phrases
- **Pattern:** Jmenuju se + name; Jsem z + city; Těší mě
- **Explanation:** 'Jmenuju se [name]' (my name is), 'Jsem z [city]' (I'm from), 'Těší mě' (Nice to meet you, literally 'it pleases me'). The formal version of 'my name is' is 'Jmenuji se'.
- **Examples:**
  - *Jmenuju se Mahesh.* — "My name is Mahesh."
  - *Jsem z Prahy.* — "I'm from Prague."

### GR-012 | Politeness words
- **Pattern:** prosím (please/you're welcome), děkuji (thank you), promiňte (excuse me)
- **Explanation:** 'prosím' means both 'please' and 'you're welcome'. 'děkuji' (or colloquially 'děkuju') means 'thank you'. 'promiňte' (formal) or 'promiň' (informal) means 'excuse me'.
- **Examples:**
  - *Kávu, prosím.* — "A coffee, please."
  - *Děkuji.* — "Thank you."

## Unit 3 — Gender & Nouns

### GR-020 | Three noun genders
- **Pattern:** Masculine (animate + inanimate), Feminine, Neuter — identified by ending
- **Explanation:** Every Czech noun has a gender, which determines endings for adjectives, pronouns, and verb agreement. Consonant ending = masculine (hrad, muž, pes), -a ending = feminine (žena, kniha, káva), -o ending = neuter (město, pivo, auto). This covers ~90% of nouns.
- **Examples:**
  - *hrad* — "castle" (masculine inanimate)
  - *žena* — "woman" (feminine)

### GR-021 | Masculine animate vs inanimate
- **Pattern:** Masculine is split: animate (people, animals) vs inanimate (objects, concepts)
- **Explanation:** Czech distinguishes between masculine animate (muž, pes, student, učitel) and masculine inanimate (hrad, dům, stůl, most). This matters for the accusative case (animate adds -a, inanimate stays the same) and for past tense plural endings.
- **Examples:**
  - *muž (animate)* — "man" (accusative: muže)
  - *hrad (inanimate)* — "castle" (accusative: hrad, unchanged)

### GR-022 | Nominative case — subject of sentence
- **Pattern:** Nominative is the dictionary form, used for the subject of sentences
- **Explanation:** The nominative case (1. pád) is the base form of a noun — the form you find in dictionaries. It is used when the noun is the subject. Questions answered: kdo? (who?) and co? (what?).
- **Examples:**
  - *Žena pije kávu.* — "The woman drinks coffee." (žena = nominative subject)
  - *Muž je učitel.* — "The man is a teacher." (muž = nominative subject)

### GR-023 | To je... (This is...) identification pattern
- **Pattern:** To je + noun — for identifying and introducing things
- **Explanation:** 'To je' (This is / That is) is the most common A1 pattern for identifying objects and people. 'To' doesn't change for gender. Plural: 'To jsou' (These are). Negation: 'To není' (That is not).
- **Examples:**
  - *To je kniha.* — "That is a book."
  - *To je muž.* — "That is a man."

### GR-024 | Profession nouns — gendered pairs
- **Pattern:** Many professions have male and female forms: -tel → -telka, -ř → -řka
- **Explanation:** Czech profession nouns have gendered forms. Common patterns: učitel/učitelka (teacher), student/studentka (student), lékař/lékařka (doctor). The female form typically adds -ka.
- **Examples:**
  - *On je učitel. Ona je učitelka.* — "He is a teacher. She is a teacher."
  - *On je student. Ona je studentka.* — "He is a student. She is a student."

## Unit 4 — Essential Verbs: být & mít

### GR-030 | být (to be) — present tense conjugation
- **Pattern:** já jsem, ty jsi, on/ona/ono je, my jsme, vy jste, oni/ony jsou
- **Explanation:** být is the most important Czech verb. The subject pronoun is usually omitted — 'Jsem student' is more natural than 'Já jsem student'.
- **Examples:**
  - *Jsem z Prahy.* — "I am from Prague."
  - *Jsi student?* — "Are you a student?"

### GR-031 | mít (to have) — present tense conjugation
- **Pattern:** já mám, ty máš, on/ona má, my máme, vy máte, oni/ony mají
- **Explanation:** Used for possession: 'Mám auto' (I have a car). Also used for age: 'Mám dvacet let' (I am twenty years old, lit. 'I have twenty years').
- **Examples:**
  - *Mám kávu.* — "I have coffee." (kávu = accusative)
  - *Máš auto?* — "Do you have a car?"

### GR-032 | Negation with být and mít
- **Pattern:** ne- prefix: nejsem, nejsi, není, nemám, nemáš, nemá
- **Explanation:** Add 'ne-' prefix to negate: nejsem (I am not), nemám (I don't have). The negative of 'je' is irregular: 'není' (not 'neje').
- **Examples:**
  - *Nejsem učitel.* — "I am not a teacher."
  - *Nemám auto.* — "I don't have a car."

### GR-033 | Question formation with být and mít
- **Pattern:** Yes/no questions by intonation; information questions with question words
- **Explanation:** Czech yes/no questions are formed by rising intonation — no word order change needed. Information questions use: kdo (who), co (what), kde (where), jak (how), odkud (from where).
- **Examples:**
  - *Jsi z Prahy?* — "Are you from Prague?"
  - *Co máš?* — "What do you have?"

## Unit 5 — Regular Verb Conjugation

### GR-040 | Verb conjugation class 1: -át verbs
- **Pattern:** Infinitive -át → present: -ám, -áš, -á, -áme, -áte, -ají
- **Explanation:** Verbs ending in -át follow this pattern. Example: dělat (to do/make) → dělám, děláš, dělá. Other common -át verbs: znát (to know), čekat (to wait), psát (to write), hrát (to play).
- **Examples:**
  - *Dělám kávu.* — "I'm making coffee."
  - *Znám Prahu.* — "I know Prague."

### GR-041 | Verb conjugation class 2: -ít/-et verbs
- **Pattern:** Infinitive -ít → present: -ím, -íš, -í, -íme, -íte, -í
- **Explanation:** Verbs ending in -ít follow this pattern. Example: mluvit (to speak) → mluvím, mluvíš, mluví. Common -ít verbs: prosit, bydlet (to live), učit (to teach).
- **Examples:**
  - *Mluvím česky.* — "I speak Czech."
  - *Prosím o kávu.* — "I'd like a coffee."

### GR-042 | Verb conjugation class 3: -et/-ět and irregular verbs
- **Pattern:** -ět verbs: -ím, -íš, -í; irregular: číst → čtu, čteš, čte, čteme, čtete, čtou
- **Explanation:** Verbs ending in -ět like rozumět (to understand) → rozumím, rozumíš. Irregular verbs include číst (to read) → čtu, čteš... and jíst (to eat) → jím, jíš, jí, jíme, jíte, jedí.
- **Examples:**
  - *Rozumím česky.* — "I understand Czech."
  - *Čtu knihu.* — "I'm reading a book."

### GR-043 | Verb negation — ne- prefix
- **Pattern:** Add ne- to the beginning of the verb: nedělám, nemluvím, nečtu
- **Explanation:** To negate any Czech verb in present tense, simply add 'ne-' before the verb. Exception: být uses nej- (nejsem, nejsi, není).
- **Examples:**
  - *Nemluvím česky.* — "I don't speak Czech."
  - *Nedělám nic.* — "I'm not doing anything."

## Unit 6 — Accusative Case

### GR-050 | Accusative case — feminine -a → -u
- **Pattern:** Feminine nouns ending in -a change to -u in accusative: káva → kávu
- **Explanation:** The accusative case (4. pád) marks the direct object. For feminine nouns ending in -a, change -a to -u: káva → kávu, žena → ženu, kniha → knihu. Question words: koho? (who?) and co? (what?).
- **Examples:**
  - *Vidím ženu.* — "I see a woman." (žena → ženu)
  - *Mám kávu.* — "I have coffee." (káva → kávu)

### GR-051 | Accusative case — masculine animate
- **Pattern:** Masculine animate nouns change ending in accusative: pes → psa, muž → muže
- **Explanation:** Masculine animate nouns (people, animals) change in accusative. Most add -a: pes → psa, student → studenta. Some change consonant + add -e: muž → muže, lékař → lékaře.
- **Examples:**
  - *Vidím muže.* — "I see a man." (muž → muže)
  - *Mám psa.* — "I have a dog." (pes → psa)

### GR-052 | Accusative case — masculine inanimate: no change
- **Pattern:** Masculine inanimate nouns stay the same in accusative: hrad → hrad, dům → dům
- **Explanation:** Masculine inanimate nouns (objects, concepts) do NOT change in accusative. The accusative form is identical to the nominative.
- **Examples:**
  - *Vidím hrad.* — "I see a castle." (hrad = same)
  - *Mám dům.* — "I have a house." (dům = same)

### GR-053 | Accusative case — neuter: no change
- **Pattern:** Neuter nouns stay the same in accusative: město → město, pivo → pivo, auto → auto
- **Explanation:** Neuter nouns (ending in -o, -í) do NOT change in accusative. Combined with masc. inanimate, only feminine -a nouns and masculine animate nouns change.
- **Examples:**
  - *Mám auto.* — "I have a car." (auto = same)
  - *Piju pivo.* — "I drink beer." (pivo = same)

## Unit 7 — Pronouns

### GR-060 | Personal pronouns — subject forms
- **Pattern:** já (I), ty (you), on (he), ona (she), ono (it), my (we), vy (you pl.), oni/ony (they)
- **Explanation:** Subject pronouns are frequently omitted — the verb ending tells you the person. 'Oni' is used for masculine animate groups; 'ony' for feminine and neuter groups.
- **Examples:**
  - *Já jsem Mahesh.* — "I am Mahesh."
  - *Ty jsi student.* — "You are a student."

### GR-061 | Possessive adjectives
- **Pattern:** můj (my), tvůj (your), jeho (his), její (her), náš (our), váš (your pl.), jejich (their)
- **Explanation:** 'jeho', 'její' and 'jejich' do NOT change for gender. 'můj' and 'tvůj' have special forms: můj (masc), moje (fem/neut); tvůj (masc), tvoje (fem/neut).
- **Examples:**
  - *To je můj pes.* — "That is my dog." (můj = masc)
  - *To je moje kniha.* — "That is my book." (moje = fem)

### GR-062 | Possessive gender agreement
- **Pattern:** můj/tvůj → moje for feminine and neuter; náš/váš → naše/vaše for feminine and neuter
- **Explanation:** Possessive adjectives must agree with the gender of the noun they modify. His (jeho), her (její), their (jejich) never change.
- **Examples:**
  - *můj bratr, moje sestra, moje auto* — "my brother, my sister, my car"
  - *tvůj pes, tvoje kniha, tvoje pivo* — "your dog, your book, your beer"

### GR-063 | Pronoun dropping — subject pronouns are optional
- **Pattern:** Czech is a pro-drop language — verb endings indicate person, so pronouns are optional
- **Explanation:** Subject pronouns can almost always be omitted because the verb ending clearly indicates the person. Pronouns are used for emphasis or contrast.
- **Examples:**
  - *Jsem z Prahy.* — "I'm from Prague." (já omitted)
  - *Jsi student?* — "Are you a student?" (ty omitted)

## Unit 8 — Family & Adjectives

### GR-070 | Family vocabulary — core members
- **Pattern:** rodina (family), matka/máma (mother), otec/táta (father), bratr (brother), sestra (sister)
- **Explanation:** máma and táta are informal/colloquial, matka and otec are formal. Key pairs: matka/máma, otec/táta, syn (son), dcera (daughter), babička (grandmother), dědeček (grandfather).
- **Examples:**
  - *Mám velkou rodinu.* — "I have a big family."
  - *Moje matka je lékařka.* — "My mother is a doctor."

### GR-071 | Adjective-noun gender agreement
- **Pattern:** Adjectives must match noun gender: masc -ý, fem -á, neuter -é
- **Explanation:** Czech adjectives change their ending to match the noun's gender. Masculine: -ý (dobrý pes), Feminine: -á (dobrá kniha), Neuter: -é (dobré pivo). The adjective always comes before the noun.
- **Examples:**
  - *velký pes* — "big dog" (masc)
  - *velká kniha* — "big book" (fem)

### GR-072 | Adjective endings — three genders
- **Pattern:** Hard adjective endings: masc -ý, fem -á, neuter -é (in nominative singular)
- **Explanation:** The basic hard adjective endings: masculine -ý (dobrý, velký, malý, starý, nový, hezký), feminine -á (dobrá, velká...), neuter -é (dobré, velké...). Soft adjectives (ending in -í) don't change: cizí stays cizí for all genders.
- **Examples:**
  - *dobrý učitel* — "good teacher" (masc anim)
  - *dobrá káva* — "good coffee" (fem)

### GR-073 | Word order: possessive + adjective + noun
- **Pattern:** Standard order: possessive adjective + descriptive adjective + noun
- **Explanation:** The descriptive adjective agrees with the noun gender, not the possessive. 'můj' is masculine, 'moje' is feminine/neuter.
- **Examples:**
  - *můj starý dům* — "my old house"
  - *moje nová kniha* — "my new book"

## Unit 9 — Numbers & Time

### GR-080 | Cardinal numbers 0–20
- **Pattern:** jedna, dva, tři, čtyři, pět, šest, sedm, osm, devět, deset, jedenáct... dvacet
- **Explanation:** Numbers 1 and 2 have gender forms — jeden (masc), jedna (fem), jedno (neut) and dva (masc), dvě (fem/neut). Numbers 11–19 end in -náct.
- **Examples:**
  - *jeden, dva, tři* — "one, two, three"
  - *čtyři, pět, šest* — "four, five, six"

### GR-081 | Numbers 21–100 and counting objects
- **Pattern:** dvacet jedna (21), třicet (30), čtyřicet (40)... sto (100)
- **Explanation:** Numbers 21–99 are 'tens + jednotka': dvacet jedna (21). When counting: 1 uses gender form, 2: dva/dvě, 3–4: plural nominative, 5+: genitive plural.
- **Examples:**
  - *dvacet jedna korun* — "21 crowns"
  - *padesát studentů* — "50 students"

### GR-082 | Telling time — Kolik je hodin?
- **Pattern:** Je jedna hodina. Jsou dvě/tři/čtyři hodiny. Je pět hodin. Use 'v' + accusative for 'at'.
- **Explanation:** For 1:00: 'Je jedna hodina.' For 2:00–4:00: 'Jsou dvě/tři/čtyři hodiny.' For 5:00+: 'Je pět hodin.' Quarter past = 'čtvrt na + next hour'. Half past = 'půl + next hour'.
- **Examples:**
  - *Kolik je hodin? Je jedna.* — "What time is it? It's one o'clock."
  - *Je půl třetí.* — "It's half past two (2:30)."

### GR-083 | Days of the week, months, and dates
- **Pattern:** Pondělí, úterý, středa... Leden, únor, březen... Dnes je pondělí. V pondělí (on Monday).
- **Explanation:** Days are NOT capitalized. 'V pondělí' = on Monday (preposition 'v' + locative). Dates use genitive: 'Je pátého ledna' (It's January 5th).
- **Examples:**
  - *Dnes je pondělí.* — "Today is Monday."
  - *V úterý mám českou hodinu.* — "On Tuesday I have Czech class."

## Unit 10 — Reflexive Verbs & Daily Routine

### GR-090 | Reflexive verbs — se (accusative reflexive)
- **Pattern:** mýt se (wash oneself), učit se (learn/study), dívat se (watch/look), smát se (laugh), bát se (be afraid)
- **Explanation:** 'Se' is the accusative reflexive particle meaning 'oneself'. The particle changes position based on sentence structure: 'Já se myju' but 'Myju se' (pronoun dropped). The se particle is NOT optional like subject pronouns.
- **Examples:**
  - *Každý den se učím česky.* — "Every day I study Czech."
  - *Ráno se myju studenou vodou.* — "In the morning I wash with cold water."

### GR-091 | Reflexive verbs — si (dative reflexive)
- **Pattern:** kupovat si (buy for oneself), vařit si (cook for oneself), číst si (read for oneself), přát si (wish)
- **Explanation:** 'Si' is the dative reflexive particle, meaning 'for/to oneself'. Compare: 'Kupuju chleba' (general) vs 'Kupuju si chleba' (for me).
- **Examples:**
  - *Kupuju si novou knihu.* — "I'm buying myself a new book."
  - *Večer si čtu knížku.* — "I read a book in the evening."

### GR-092 | Reflexive verb conjugation — present tense
- **Pattern:** já se myju, ty se myješ, on/ona/ono se myje, my se myjeme, vy se myjete, oni se myjou
- **Explanation:** Reflexive verbs conjugate like regular verbs, with se/si moving to 2nd position. For negation: the particle stays in 2nd position and the verb gets ne-: 'Já se neučím'.
- **Examples:**
  - *Já se myju, ty se myješ, on se myje.* — "I wash, you wash, he washes."
  - *My se učíme česky.* — "We are learning Czech."

### GR-093 | Time adverbs for daily routine
- **Pattern:** ráno (morning), dopoledne (late morning), v poledne (at noon), odpoledne (afternoon), večer (evening), v noci (at night), každý den (every day), obvykle (usually), často (often), někdy (sometimes), nikdy (never)
- **Explanation:** Time adverb usually comes first or second in the sentence. Frequency adverbs: vždycky (always), obvykle (usually), často (often), někdy (sometimes), nikdy (never).
- **Examples:**
  - *Ráno vstávám v šest hodin.* — "I get up at six in the morning."
  - *Večer se dívám na televizi.* — "In the evening I watch TV."

## Unit 11 — Food & Restaurants

### GR-100 | jíst and pít — eating and drinking verbs
- **Pattern:** já jím, ty jíš, on jí, my jíme, vy jíte, oni jedí || já piju, ty piješ, on pije, my pijeme, vy pijete, oni pijou
- **Explanation:** 'Jíst' (to eat) is irregular: jím, jíš, jí, jíme, jíte, jedí. 'Pít' (to drink) is semi-regular. Both take accusative objects.
- **Examples:**
  - *Jím chleba se sýrem.* — "I eat bread with cheese."
  - *Co jíš? Jím polévku.* — "What are you eating? I'm eating soup."

### GR-101 | Food vocabulary — gender and accusative
- **Pattern:** chléb (masc inan, acc=chleba), polévka (fem, acc=polévku), máslo (neut, acc=máslo), vejce (neut, acc=vejce), sýr (masc inan, acc=sýr)
- **Explanation:** Food nouns follow gender rules for accusative. Feminine -a→-u (polévka→polévku). Masculine inanimate: usually no change. Neuter: no change. When ordering: 'Dám si...' + accusative.
- **Examples:**
  - *Dám si guláš a pivo.* — "I'll have goulash and a beer."
  - *K snídani mám chleba s máslem.* — "For breakfast I have bread with butter."

### GR-102 | Ordering at a restaurant
- **Pattern:** Dám si... (I'll have...). Chtěl bych... (I would like...). Ještě něco? (Anything else?). Platit! (The bill!). Účet, prosím.
- **Explanation:** 'Dám si...' + accusative is most common. 'Chtěl bych...' is more polite. Waiter asks: 'Co si dáte?' or 'Ještě něco?'. For the bill: 'Platit!', 'Účet, prosím', or 'Můžu zaplatit?'.
- **Examples:**
  - *Dám si smažený sýr a hranolky.* — "I'll have fried cheese and fries."
  - *Platit! Můžu kartou?* — "The bill! Can I pay by card?"

### GR-103 | Quantities and partitive genitive with food
- **Pattern:** trochu (a little), hodně (a lot), kilo (kilogram), litr (liter), kousek (piece)
- **Explanation:** After quantity words, use the genitive case: 'trochu mléka' (a little milk), 'hodně jídla' (a lot of food), 'kilo brambor' (a kilo of potatoes), 'litr vína' (a liter of wine).
- **Examples:**
  - *Dám si trochu polévky.* — "I'll have a little soup."
  - *Kupte kilo jablek.* — "Buy a kilo of apples."

## Unit 12 — Shopping & Prices

### GR-110 | Kolik to stojí? — asking and stating prices
- **Pattern:** Kolik to stojí? Stojí to... korun. jedna koruna, dvě/tři/čtyři koruny, pět+ korun
- **Explanation:** 'Kolik to stojí?' = How much does it cost? Price follows the number rule: 1 koruna, 2–4 koruny, 5+ korun. Czech crown = 'koruna' (Kč).
- **Examples:**
  - *Kolik to stojí? — Stojí to třicet korun.* — "How much does it cost? — It costs 30 crowns."
  - *To je moc drahé!* — "That's too expensive!"

### GR-111 | Shopping — chtít and kupovat + accusative
- **Pattern:** Chci... / Chtěl bych... + accusative. Kupuju... + accusative. Můžu...? (Can I...?)
- **Explanation:** Key verbs: 'chci' + accusative, 'kupuju', 'hledám' (I'm looking for). More polite: 'Chtěl bych...'. Common: 'Můžu se podívat?' (Can I look?), 'Vezmu si to' (I'll take it).
- **Examples:**
  - *Hledám nový svetr.* — "I'm looking for a new sweater."
  - *Vezmu si to. Kolik to stojí?* — "I'll take it. How much does it cost?"

### GR-112 | Clothes vocabulary and colors
- **Pattern:** svetr (sweater), kalhoty (pants), košile (shirt), bunda (jacket), boty (shoes), šaty (dress). Colors: červený, modrý, zelený, žlutý, černý, bílý.
- **Explanation:** Clothes have genders: svetr (masc), košile (fem), tričko (neut). Kalhoty, boty, šaty are plural only. Colors agree with noun: červený svetr, červená košile, červené tričko.
- **Examples:**
  - *Mám modrý svetr a černé kalhoty.* — "I have a blue sweater and black pants."
  - *Jakou barvu chcete? — Červenou.* — "What color do you want? — Red."

### GR-113 | Comparisons — větší, menší, lepší, horší
- **Pattern:** velký→větší, malý→menší, dobrý→lepší, špatný→horší, drahý→dražší, levný→levnější
- **Explanation:** Comparative adjectives for shopping: drahý→dražší (more expensive), levný→levnější (cheaper). Use 'než' (than): 'Tento svetr je levnější než tamten.' Superlative adds nej-: nejlevnější.
- **Examples:**
  - *Tento svetr je levnější.* — "This sweater is cheaper."
  - *Máte větší velikost?* — "Do you have a bigger size?"

## Unit 13 — Hobbies & Likes

### GR-120 | mít rád — expressing likes
- **Pattern:** Mám rád/ráda... + accusative. Nemám rád... . Máš rád...?
- **Explanation:** 'Mít rád' conjugates like 'mít': mám rád (masc), mám ráda (fem), máš rád/ráda... The object must be in accusative: 'Mám rád čokoládu'. For activities, use 'rád + verb': 'Rád čtu'.
- **Examples:**
  - *Mám rád české pivo.* — "I like Czech beer. (male)"
  - *Nemám rád zimu.* — "I don't like winter."

### GR-121 | rád + verb — liking activities
- **Pattern:** Rád/Ráda + conjugated verb. Rád čtu, rád sportuju, ráda vařím. Radši... (prefer).
- **Explanation:** 'Rád' (masc)/'Ráda' (fem) + conjugated verb = liking an activity. 'Radši' = rather/prefer. Negation: 'Nerad/Nerada + verb' = I don't like doing.
- **Examples:**
  - *Rád sportuju a plavu.* — "I like to do sports and swim."
  - *Co ráda děláš ve volném čase?* — "What do you like to do in your free time?"

### GR-122 | Hobbies & free time vocabulary
- **Pattern:** číst (read), sportovat (do sports), plavat (swim), běhat (run), vařit (cook), cestovat (travel), hrát na... (play instrument), dívat se na... (watch)
- **Explanation:** For instruments: 'hrát na + accusative' — 'Hraju na kytaru'. For watching: 'dívat se na + accusative'. Free time = 'volný čas'. Hobby = 'koníček'.
- **Examples:**
  - *Ve volném čase rád čtu.* — "In my free time I like to read."
  - *Hraju na kytaru a zpívám.* — "I play guitar and sing."

### GR-123 | Adverbs of frequency
- **Pattern:** vždycky (always), často (often), občas (sometimes), málokdy (rarely), nikdy (never)
- **Explanation:** Frequency adverbs typically come in 2nd position. With 'nikdy', use a NEGATED verb: 'Nikdy nevařím' (double negation is standard Czech). 'Jak často?' = How often?
- **Examples:**
  - *Často chodím do kina.* — "I often go to the cinema."
  - *Nikdy necvičím ráno.* — "I never exercise in the morning."

## Unit 14 — Directions & Places

### GR-130 | Kde je...? — asking where things are (locative intro)
- **Pattern:** Kde je...? (Where is...?). Je v... / na... (It's in/at...). Tady, tam, nahoře, dole.
- **Explanation:** 'V' + locative = in (v Praze). 'Na' + locative = at/on (na nádraží). Key location words: tady (here), tam (there), vedle (next to), naproti (opposite), blízko (near), daleko (far). Directions: rovně (straight), doleva (left), doprava (right).
- **Examples:**
  - *Kde je nádraží? — Je tam, vlevo.* — "Where is the station? — It's there, on the left."
  - *Kde bydlíš? — Bydlím v Praze.* — "Where do you live? — I live in Prague."

### GR-131 | Kam? — motion with accusative vs location with locative
- **Pattern:** Kam jdeš? (Where are you going?) vs Kde jsi? (Where are you?) — Jdu do školy vs Jsem ve škole.
- **Explanation:** Czech distinguishes MOTION (where to?) from LOCATION (where?). 'Kam?' uses 'do' + genitive for destination or 'na' + accusative. 'Kde?' uses 'v/ve' + locative or 'na' + locative. Master this contrast.
- **Examples:**
  - *Jdu do školy.* vs *Jsem ve škole.* — "I'm going to school." vs "I'm at school."
  - *Jedu na nádraží.* vs *Jsem na nádraží.* — "I'm going to the station." vs "I'm at the station."

### GR-132 | Transport and movement verbs — jít vs jet
- **Pattern:** jít (go on foot), jet (go by vehicle), chodit (walk regularly), jezdit (travel regularly)
- **Explanation:** 'Jít' = go on foot (jdu, jdeš, jde). 'Jet' = go by vehicle (jedu, jedeš, jede). Habitual: 'chodit' (walk regularly), 'jezdit' (travel regularly). Transport: autobusem, tramvají, metrem, autem, na kole, pěšky.
- **Examples:**
  - *Jdu pěšky do školy.* — "I walk to school."
  - *Jedeme autobusem do centra.* — "We go by bus to the center."

### GR-133 | Places in town vocabulary
- **Pattern:** obchod (shop), restaurace, nádraží (station), nemocnice (hospital), škola, pošta (post office), banka, lékárna (pharmacy), kino, divadlo (theater)
- **Explanation:** Masculine: obchod, park, most. Feminine: restaurace, nemocnice, škola, banka, lékárna, pošta. Neuter: nádraží, náměstí, kino, divadlo, letiště. Directions: 'Je na rohu', 'Je vedle banky' (genitive), 'Je naproti kinu' (dative).
- **Examples:**
  - *Kde je nejbližší lékárna?* — "Where is the nearest pharmacy?"
  - *Restaurace je naproti kinu.* — "The restaurant is opposite the cinema."

## Unit 15 — Weather & Seasons

### GR-140 | Weather expressions — počasí
- **Pattern:** Je... (It is...). Prší. Sněží. Svítí slunce. Je zima/teplo/horko/chladno.
- **Explanation:** Weather uses 'Je' + adjective/adverb: 'Je hezky', 'Je zima' (cold), 'Je teplo' (warm), 'Je horko' (hot). Verbs: 'Prší' (raining), 'Sněží' (snowing), 'Svítí slunce' (sun shining). 'Jaké je počasí?' = What's the weather like?
- **Examples:**
  - *Jaké je dnes počasí? — Je hezky.* — "What's the weather like today? — It's nice."
  - *Prší. Vezmi si deštník!* — "It's raining. Take an umbrella!"

### GR-141 | Seasons — roční období
- **Pattern:** jaro (spring), léto (summer), podzim (autumn), zima (winter). Na jaře, v létě, na podzim, v zimě.
- **Explanation:** Seasons: jaro (neut), léto (neut), podzim (masc), zima (fem). 'In' + season: 'na jaře', 'v létě', 'na podzim', 'v zimě'. Note jaro uses 'na' + locative, not 'v'.
- **Examples:**
  - *Na jaře kvetou květiny.* — "In spring, flowers bloom."
  - *V létě jezdíme k moři.* — "In summer we go to the sea."

### GR-142 | Future tense intro — budu + infinitive
- **Pattern:** budu, budeš, bude, budeme, budete, budou + infinitive
- **Explanation:** Future of imperfective verbs = future of být + infinitive. 'Budu dělat' (I will do). The future of 'být' itself: budu, budeš, bude, budeme, budete, budou.
- **Examples:**
  - *Zítra budu v práci.* — "Tomorrow I will be at work."
  - *V létě budeme cestovat.* — "In summer we will travel."

### GR-143 | Past tense intro — byl, měl, dělal
- **Pattern:** Byl jsem, byl jsi, byl... Měl jsem... Dělal jsem... The -l participle + být auxiliary.
- **Explanation:** Past tense uses -l participle + auxiliary 'být' (except 3rd person drops auxiliary). 'Byl jsem v Praze' (I was in Prague). 'Měl jsem se dobře' (I had a good time).
- **Examples:**
  - *Byl jsem v Praze minulý týden.* — "I was in Prague last week. (male)"
  - *Byla jsem v kině včera.* — "I was at the cinema yesterday. (female)"

## Unit 16 — Genitive Case

### GR-150 | Genitive — masculine endings
- **Pattern:** Masculine animate: -a (bratr→bratra, učitel→učitele). Masculine inanimate: -u/-e (dům→domu, čaj→čaje).
- **Explanation:** Genitive expresses possession (dům bratra = brother's house), origin (z Prahy), and follows certain prepositions (bez, do, od, z/ze). Animate adds -a, inanimate typically adds -u.
- **Examples:**
  - *To je auto mého bratra.* — "That is my brother's car."
  - *Jsem z Prahy.* — "I am from Prague."

### GR-151 | Genitive — feminine and neuter endings
- **Pattern:** Feminine: -y/-i (žena→ženy, ulice→ulice). Neuter: -a/-e (město→města, moře→moře).
- **Explanation:** Feminine genitive: -a stems → -y (žena→ženy), soft stems stay -e/-i (ulice→ulice). Neuter: hard stems -o→-a (město→města), soft stems → -e (moře→moře).
- **Examples:**
  - *Bydlím u sestry.* — "I live at my sister's."
  - *Jdu do školy.* — "I'm going to school."

### GR-152 | Genitive plural and quantities
- **Pattern:** With numbers 5+: pět mužů, šest žen, deset měst. Quantity words (hodně, málo, trochu) + genitive.
- **Explanation:** After numbers 5+, use genitive plural: pět studentů. Quantity expressions: hodně + gen (a lot of), málo + gen (little), trochu + gen (a bit). Genitive plural endings: masc -ů, fem -ø, neut -ø.
- **Examples:**
  - *Mám hodně práce.* — "I have a lot of work."
  - *Pět studentů je ve třídě.* — "Five students are in the classroom."

### GR-153 | Negation and genitive
- **Pattern:** Accusative objects become genitive when negated: Mám čas → Nemám času. Vidím knihu → Nevidím knihy.
- **Explanation:** When a verb that normally takes an accusative object is negated, the object often shifts to genitive. Common with 'mít' (Mám peníze → Nemám peněz). More common in formal/written Czech.
- **Examples:**
  - *Nemám peněz.* — "I don't have money."
  - *Nevidím žádného problému.* — "I don't see any problem."

## Unit 17 — Dative Case

### GR-160 | Dative — endings and indirect objects
- **Pattern:** Masc -ovi/-u: bratr→bratrovi, pán→pánovi. Fem -e/-i: žena→ženě, ulice→ulici. Neut -u/-i: město→městu, nádraží→nádraží.
- **Explanation:** Dative marks the indirect object (to whom? for whom?). 'Dávám knihu bratrovi' (I give a book TO my brother). Masculine animate: typically -ovi; feminine: -e for hard stems; neuter: -u for hard stems.
- **Examples:**
  - *Dávám dárek mamince.* — "I give a gift to mom."
  - *Pomáhám kamarádovi.* — "I help my friend."

### GR-161 | Verbs requiring dative
- **Pattern:** Pomáhat + dat, rozumět + dat, telefonovat + dat, věřit + dat, radit + dat.
- **Explanation:** Several common verbs require dative: pomáhat (help), rozumět (understand), telefonovat (call), věřit (believe), radit (advise). These take dative even though English uses direct objects.
- **Examples:**
  - *Rozumíš češtině?* — "Do you understand Czech?"
  - *Telefonuju matce každý den.* — "I call my mother every day."

### GR-162 | Líbit se and chutnat — liking in dative
- **Pattern:** Líbí se mi... (I like...), Chutná mi... (I like the taste of...). The liked thing is the subject (nominative), the person is dative.
- **Explanation:** With 'líbit se', the thing liked is the subject and the person who likes it is in dative. 'Líbí se mi Praha' = literally 'Prague pleases itself to me'. Same with 'chutnat'.
- **Examples:**
  - *Líbí se ti Praha?* — "Do you like Prague?"
  - *Chutná mi české pivo.* — "I like the taste of Czech beer."

### GR-163 | Impersonal expressions with dative
- **Pattern:** Je mi zima/teplo/dobře/špatně. Je mi 20 let. Jde mi to. Záleží mi na tom.
- **Explanation:** Czech uses dative for impersonal expressions: Je mi zima (I'm cold), Je mi dobře (I feel good), Je mi líto (I'm sorry). Age is also dative: Je mi 20 let.
- **Examples:**
  - *Je mi zima. Můžu zavřít okno?* — "I'm cold. Can I close the window?"
  - *Je mi dvacet pět let.* — "I'm 25 years old."

## Unit 18 — Locative & Instrumental Cases

### GR-170 | Locative case — v/na + locative
- **Pattern:** v + loc: v Praze, v pokoji, v kině. na + loc: na náměstí, na nádraží, na univerzitě.
- **Explanation:** The locative case is used ONLY after prepositions. Primary: v/ve (in) and na (on/at). Also with o (about): 'Mluvím o práci'. With po (after): 'po obědě'.
- **Examples:**
  - *Bydlím v Praze.* — "I live in Prague."
  - *Kniha je na stole.* — "The book is on the table."

### GR-171 | Instrumental case — means and accompaniment
- **Pattern:** s kým/čím? (with whom/what?). Means: autem, vlakem, metrem. Professions: pracovat jako... (+ nom or instr). Time: na jaře → na jaře, ale v zimě → zimou.
- **Explanation:** Instrumental expresses means: 'jedu autem' (by car). Accompaniment: 's + instrumental': 'Jdu s kamarádem'. Masculine ending: -em; Feminine: -ou; Neuter: -em.
- **Examples:**
  - *Jedu do práce autem.* — "I go to work by car."
  - *Jdu s kamarádkou do kina.* — "I'm going to the cinema with a friend."

### GR-172 | Locative — o + locative (about)
- **Pattern:** o kom/čem? Mluvit o..., přemýšlet o..., kniha o..., film o..., starat se o...
- **Explanation:** 'O' + locative = 'about'. Key verbs: mluvit o (talk about), přemýšlet o (think about), číst o (read about), psát o (write about). Also: kniha o Praze (a book about Prague).
- **Examples:**
  - *O čem mluvíte? — Mluvíme o dovolené.* — "What are you talking about? — We're talking about vacation."
  - *Přemýšlím o tom problému.* — "I'm thinking about that problem."

### GR-173 | Instrumental plural and time expressions
- **Pattern:** Plural instrumental: -y/-i/-ami (muži, ženami, městy). Time: před rokem (a year ago, instr).
- **Explanation:** Instrumental plural: masc -y/-i (muži, studenty), fem -ami (ženami), neut -y (městy). Time: před + instrumental = ago (před rokem = a year ago).
- **Examples:**
  - *Bydlím s rodiči.* — "I live with my parents."
  - *Před rokem jsem byl v Praze.* — "A year ago I was in Prague."

## Unit 19 — Past Tense

### GR-180 | Past tense — formation and gender agreement
- **Pattern:** -l (masc), -la (fem), -lo (neut), -li (masc pl), -ly (fem/neut pl). Auxiliary: jsem/jsi/jsme/jste (3rd person omitted).
- **Explanation:** Past tense = -l participle + být auxiliary. Gender matters: masc -l, fem -la, neut -lo. Plural: at least one male → -li, otherwise -ly. The 3rd person drops the auxiliary.
- **Examples:**
  - *Včera jsem byl v kině.* — "Yesterday I was at the cinema. (male)"
  - *Ona studovala v Praze.* — "She studied in Prague."

### GR-181 | Motion verbs in past tense
- **Pattern:** jít→šel/šla/šlo/šli/šly. jet→jel/jela/jelo/jeli/jely. The auxiliary follows the -l participle: šel jsem, šla jsi.
- **Explanation:** Motion verbs have irregular past participles: jít → šel/šla/šlo/šli/šly. Jet → jel/jela/jelo/jeli/jely. Also: jíst → jedl, číst → četl.
- **Examples:**
  - *Kam jsi šel včera? — Šel jsem do práce.* — "Where did you go yesterday? — I went to work."
  - *Jela jste vlakem? — Ano, jela.* — "Did you go by train? (fem) — Yes, I did."

### GR-182 | Perfective vs imperfective — aspect introduction
- **Pattern:** Imperfective: dělat, psát, číst, kupovat (process/ongoing). Perfective: udělat, napsat, přečíst, koupit (completed action).
- **Explanation:** Czech verbs come in aspect pairs. Imperfective = ongoing/repeated (dělám = I am doing). Perfective = completed (udělám = I will do/get it done). Perfective present form = future meaning.
- **Examples:**
  - *Psal jsem dopis celé odpoledne.* — "I was writing the letter all afternoon. (imperfective)"
  - *Napsal jsem dopis za hodinu.* — "I wrote the letter in an hour. (perfective)"

### GR-183 | Time expressions for past tense
- **Pattern:** včera (yesterday), předevčírem (day before yesterday), minulý týden/měsíc/rok (last week/month/year), před rokem (a year ago).
- **Explanation:** Key past time markers: včera, předevčírem, minulý týden/měsíc/rok, před + instrumental (ago). These typically appear at the beginning or in 2nd position of sentences.
- **Examples:**
  - *Včera jsem potkal starého kamaráda.* — "Yesterday I met an old friend."
  - *Minulý týden jsme byli v Brně.* — "Last week we were in Brno."

## Unit 20 — Future & Conditional

### GR-190 | Future tense — imperfective (budu + infinitive)
- **Pattern:** budu/budeš/bude/budeme/budete/budou + infinitive. Budu pracovat, budeš číst, budeme cestovat.
- **Explanation:** Imperfective future = budu + infinitive. Used for ongoing/repeated/or descriptive future actions. Common time markers: zítra (tomorrow), příští týden (next week), brzy (soon).
- **Examples:**
  - *Zítra budu pracovat celý den.* — "Tomorrow I will work all day."
  - *Příští rok budeme cestovat po Evropě.* — "Next year we will travel around Europe."

### GR-191 | Perfective future — present form = future meaning
- **Pattern:** napíšu, přečtu, udělám, koupím — perfective verbs in present conjugation express FUTURE.
- **Explanation:** Perfective verbs conjugated in present form express future meaning. 'Napíšu dopis' = I will write a letter (future, not 'I am writing'). Compare: 'Píšu dopis' (present) vs 'Napíšu dopis' (future).
- **Examples:**
  - *Napíšu ti email zítra.* — "I'll write you an email tomorrow."
  - *Koupíš mi kávu?* — "Will you buy me a coffee?"

### GR-192 | Conditional — bych, bys, by
- **Pattern:** Já bych, ty bys, on/ona/ono by, my bychom, vy byste, oni by. + past participle (-l/-la/-lo).
- **Explanation:** Conditional = bych/bys/by + -l participle. 'Chtěl bych' (I would like), 'Měl bys' (you should), 'Mohli bychom' (we could). Expresses wishes, polite requests, hypothetical situations.
- **Examples:**
  - *Chtěl bych kávu, prosím.* — "I would like a coffee, please. (male)"
  - *Měl bys víc spát.* — "You should sleep more."

### GR-193 | Kdyby — conditional 'if'
- **Pattern:** Kdybych, kdybys, kdyby, kdybychom, kdybyste, kdyby + -l participle.
- **Explanation:** 'Kdyby' (if) + conditional forms. The verb in the kdyby-clause is always in past tense. Main clause uses bych + past participle.
- **Examples:**
  - *Kdybych měl čas, šel bych do kina.* — "If I had time, I would go to the cinema."
  - *Kdybys chtěl, můžeme jít spolu.* — "If you want, we can go together."

## Unit 21 — Comparison

### GR-200 | Comparative adjectives — regular formation
- **Pattern:** -ější/-ejší (mladý→mladší, starý→starší, chytrý→chytřejší), -ší (čistý→čistší), or -í (tichý→tišší).
- **Explanation:** Regular comparatives add -(ěj)ší to the stem. Stem often changes: drahý → dražší. Use 'než' (than): 'Praha je větší než Brno'. Comparatives decline like soft adjectives.
- **Examples:**
  - *Praha je větší než Brno.* — "Prague is bigger than Brno."
  - *Můj bratr je mladší než já.* — "My brother is younger than me."

### GR-201 | Irregular comparative adjectives
- **Pattern:** dobrý→lepší, špatný→horší, velký→větší, malý→menší, dlouhý→delší, krátký→kratší, vysoký→vyšší, nízký→nižší.
- **Explanation:** Several common adjectives have irregular comparatives that must be memorized. These are high-frequency words essential for everyday conversation.
- **Examples:**
  - *Tohle je lepší varianta.* — "This is a better option."
  - *Jeho byt je větší než můj.* — "His apartment is bigger than mine."

### GR-202 | Superlative — nej- prefix
- **Pattern:** nej- + comparative: nejlepší, největší, nejdražší. Declines like a soft adjective.
- **Explanation:** Superlative = prefix nej- + comparative. 'Nejlepší' (best), 'nejhorší' (worst), 'největší' (biggest). With 'z' + genitive: 'nejlepší z restaurací' (the best of restaurants).
- **Examples:**
  - *To je nejlepší restaurace ve městě.* — "That's the best restaurant in the city."
  - *On je nejchytřejší student ve třídě.* — "He's the cleverest student in the class."

### GR-203 | Comparative and superlative adverbs
- **Pattern:** rychle→rychleji→nejrychleji, dobře→lépe→nejlépe, špatně→hůře→nejhůře, často→častěji→nejčastěji.
- **Explanation:** Adverb comparatives: -eji/-ěji. Irregulars: dobře→lépe (well→better), špatně→hůře (worse), hodně→více (more), málo→méně (less), brzy→dříve (earlier), dlouho→déle (longer).
- **Examples:**
  - *Mluvíš česky lépe než já.* — "You speak Czech better than me."
  - *Přijď dříve, prosím.* — "Come earlier, please."

## Unit 22 — Complex Sentences

### GR-210 | že — 'that' clauses
- **Pattern:** Vím, že... Myslím, že... Řekl, že... + normal word order after že.
- **Explanation:** 'Že' (that) introduces a subordinate clause with normal word order — verb does NOT go to the end (unlike German). Common: říct (say), myslet (think), vědět (know), slyšet (hear).
- **Examples:**
  - *Myslím, že máš pravdu.* — "I think that you're right."
  - *Vím, že je to těžké.* — "I know that it's difficult."

### GR-211 | Protože and když — cause and time clauses
- **Pattern:** Protože + clause (because). Když + clause (when/whenever). Word order: standard SVO.
- **Explanation:** 'Protože' (because) introduces a reason clause. 'Když' (when/whenever) for factual/repeated events. Both use standard word order. 'Jestli/zda' = if/whether for indirect questions.
- **Examples:**
  - *Nepřišel, protože byl nemocný.* — "He didn't come because he was sick."
  - *Když mám čas, rád čtu.* — "When I have time, I like to read."

### GR-212 | Aby — purpose clauses
- **Pattern:** Abych, abys, aby, abychom, abyste, aby + -l participle. Aby = 'so that / in order to'.
- **Explanation:** 'Aby' introduces purpose clauses using the conditional forms + -l participle. When the subject is the same in both clauses, Czech prefers aby over the infinitive.
- **Examples:**
  - *Učím se česky, abych mohl pracovat v Praze.* — "I'm learning Czech so that I can work in Prague."
  - *Chci, abys mi pomohl.* — "I want you to help me."

### GR-213 | Word order in complex sentences
- **Pattern:** Main clause: SVO. After že/když/protože: SVO (normal). After aby/kdyby: S + conditional + -l participle. Clitics (se, si, mi, ti, ho) in 2nd position.
- **Explanation:** Main clauses: SVO with clitics in 2nd position. After že/protože/když: normal word order. After aby/kdyby: aby/kdyby form takes 2nd position. The reflexive se/si is absorbed: aby + se → aby ses.
- **Examples:**
  - *Vím, že se učíš česky.* — "I know that you're studying Czech."
  - *Chci, aby ses víc učil.* — "I want you to study more."

## Unit 23 — Modal Verbs

### GR-220 | muset — must / have to (obligation)
- **Pattern:** muset + infinitive — expresses strong obligation or necessity
- **Explanation:** Conjugates: musím, musíš, musí, musíme, musíte, musí. Negation: nemuset = 'don't have to' (lack of obligation, NOT 'must not').
- **Examples:**
  - *Musím jít do práce.* — "I must go to work."
  - *Musíš se učit.* — "You have to study."

### GR-221 | moct — can / be able to (ability)
- **Pattern:** moct + infinitive — expresses ability or permission
- **Explanation:** Conjugates: můžu/mohu, můžeš, může, můžeme, můžete, mohou. 'můžu' is more common in speech, 'mohu' in formal writing. Negation: nemoci = 'cannot'.
- **Examples:**
  - *Můžu přijít zítra.* — "I can come tomorrow."
  - *Můžeš mi pomoci?* — "Can you help me?"

### GR-222 | smět — be allowed to (permission)
- **Pattern:** smět + infinitive — expresses permission or prohibition
- **Explanation:** Conjugates: smím, smíš, smí, smíme, smíte, smí. Negation: nesmět = 'must not' (prohibition). Key distinction: nesmět ≠ nemuset. Nesmět = you must NOT, nemuset = don't have to.
- **Examples:**
  - *Smím vstoupit?* — "May I come in?"
  - *Nesmíš tu parkovat.* — "You must not park here."

### GR-223 | Modal verb + infinitive construction
- **Pattern:** Conjugated modal + infinitive of main verb — modal is conjugated, main verb stays in infinitive
- **Explanation:** All Czech modal verbs (muset, moct, smět, chtít) follow the same pattern: modal is conjugated, main verb stays in infinitive. Modal is always in second position.
- **Examples:**
  - *Musím pracovat.* — "I must work."
  - *Chci se učit česky.* — "I want to learn Czech."

### GR-224 | Expressing obligation, permission, and prohibition
- **Pattern:** muset (obligation), moct (ability/permission), smět (formal permission), nesmět (prohibition)
- **Explanation:** Czech distinguishes three types: OBLIGATION (muset), ABILITY/PERMISSION (moct), FORMAL PERMISSION/PROHIBITION (smět/nesmět). 'Nesmíš' = must not, 'Nemusíš' = don't have to. Formal rules: Je dovoleno, Je zakázáno.
- **Examples:**
  - *Musíte mít průkaz.* — "You must have an ID. (obligation)"
  - *Nesmíte fotit v muzeu.* — "You must not take photos in the museum. (prohibition)"

## Unit 24 — Health & Body

### GR-230 | Bolí mě — expressing pain with body parts
- **Pattern:** bolí + accusative pronoun (mě, tě, ho, ji, nás, vás, je) + body part (nominative)
- **Explanation:** 'Bolí' is always 3rd person singular, followed by accusative pronoun + body part in nominative. For plural body parts (eyes, ears, hands), use bolí + pronoun + plural nominative.
- **Examples:**
  - *Bolí mě hlava.* — "My head hurts. / I have a headache."
  - *Bolí tě krk?* — "Does your throat hurt?"

### GR-231 | cítit se — feeling unwell and reflexive health verbs
- **Pattern:** cítit se + adverb (dobře, špatně, unavený, nemocný)
- **Explanation:** 'Cítit se' means 'to feel' (health/state). Conjugates: cítím se, cítíš se... Common: cítit se dobře/špatně/unavený/nemocný. For fever: 'mít teplotu'.
- **Examples:**
  - *Cítím se špatně.* — "I feel unwell."
  - *Jak se cítíš?* — "How do you feel?"

### GR-232 | At the doctor — questions and medical phrases
- **Pattern:** Common doctor-patient exchanges: Co vás trápí? / Boli mě... / Ukážete jazyk? / Vypijte tohle.
- **Explanation:** Doctor: 'Co vás trápí?' (What's bothering you?), 'Ukážete jazyk?' Patient: 'Bolí mě hlava/krk/břicho', 'Mám teplotu/kašel/rýmu', 'Potřebuji recept'. Pharmacy: 'Kde je lékárna?'
- **Examples:**
  - *Co vás trápí?* — "What's bothering you?"
  - *Bolí mě krk a mám teplotu.* — "My throat hurts and I have a fever."

## Unit 25 — Work & Study

### GR-240 | Profession with být + instrumental
- **Pattern:** být (conjugated) + noun in instrumental case — jsem učitelem, je lékařem
- **Explanation:** Professions expressed with být + instrumental: 'jsem učitelem' (I am a teacher). Instrumental endings: masc -em/-ou, fem -ou, neut -em. Nominative also works in everyday speech (jsem učitel).
- **Examples:**
  - *Jsem učitelem.* — "I am a teacher."
  - *On je lékařem v nemocnici.* — "He is a doctor at the hospital."

### GR-241 | pracovat jako — describing one's job
- **Pattern:** pracovat jako + nominative noun — Pracuji jako učitel. / Dělá co + accusative
- **Explanation:** Two constructions: (1) 'pracovat jako + nominative' (Pracuji jako učitel), (2) 'dělat + accusative' (Dělám učitele). Ask: 'Co děláš?' or 'Čím se živíš?' or 'Jaké je tvoje povolání?'
- **Examples:**
  - *Pracuji jako programátor.* — "I work as a programmer."
  - *Co děláš? — Dělám učitele.* — "What do you do? — I'm a teacher."

### GR-242 | Education vocabulary and school-related grammar
- **Pattern:** učit se (learn), studovat (study), zkouška (exam), předmět (subject) — common school constructions
- **Explanation:** 'Učit se' (learn, reflexive) vs 'učit' (teach, no se). 'Studovat' = study at an institution. Common: učit se česky, studovat na univerzitě, dělat/udělat zkoušku. Subjects: předmět.
- **Examples:**
  - *Učím se česky.* — "I am learning Czech."
  - *Studuji na Karlově univerzitě.* — "I study at Charles University."

## Unit 26 — Housing

### GR-250 | Housing vocabulary — rooms and furniture genders
- **Pattern:** Room names by gender: byt (masc inan), kuchyně (fem), ložnice (fem), koupelna (fem); furniture: stůl (masc inan), židle (fem), postel (fem), skříň (fem)
- **Explanation:** Rooms: byt (masc), pokoj (masc), kuchyně (fem), ložnice (fem), koupelna (fem), předsíň (fem). Furniture: stůl (masc), židle (fem), postel (fem), skříň (fem), lednice (fem), sporák (masc). Gender determines adjective agreement.
- **Examples:**
  - *Mám nový byt.* — "I have a new apartment."
  - *Naše kuchyně je velká.* — "Our kitchen is big."

### GR-251 | Prepositions of location in housing
- **Pattern:** v/ve (in) + locative, na (on) + locative, u (by/at) + genitive — location in rooms
- **Explanation:** 'v/ve + locative' (v kuchyni), 'na + locative' (na stole), 'u + genitive' (u okna), 'před + instrumental' (in front of), 'za + instrumental' (behind), 'mezi + instrumental' (between).
- **Examples:**
  - *Kniha je na stole.* — "The book is on the table."
  - *Stůl je v kuchyni.* — "The table is in the kitchen."

### GR-252 | Renting and buying — pronajmout si, koupit si
- **Pattern:** pronajmout si (rent for oneself) + accusative, koupit si (buy for oneself) + accusative — reflexive possessive svůj for one's own
- **Explanation:** Reflexive 'si' indicates for yourself: 'pronajmout si byt', 'koupit si dům'. Vocabulary: nájem (rent), nájemník (tenant), majitel (owner). 'Hledám byt k pronájmu', 'Kolik stojí nájem?'
- **Examples:**
  - *Pronajmul jsem si byt v Praze.* — "I rented an apartment in Prague."
  - *Chci si koupit dům.* — "I want to buy a house."

## Unit 27 — Motion & Aspect

### GR-260 | Motion verbs — jít vs jet vs chodit vs jezdit
- **Pattern:** jít/jet = one-time motion (on foot/by vehicle), chodit/jezdit = habitual/repeated motion
- **Explanation:** Czech pairs: jít (go on foot one time) vs chodit (habitual), jet (by vehicle one time) vs jezdit (habitual). 'Jdu do práce' (right now) vs 'Chodím do práce' (every day). Other pairs: běžet/běhat, letět/létat.
- **Examples:**
  - *Jdu do obchodu.* — "I'm going to the store. (right now)"
  - *Chodím do školy každý den.* — "I go to school every day. (habitual)"

### GR-261 | Prefixed motion verbs — přijít, odejít, dojít, vyjít, projít
- **Pattern:** prefix + jít/jet = directional specification of motion
- **Explanation:** Prefixes: při- (arrive: přijít), od- (leave: odejít), do- (reach: dojít), vy- (exit: vyjít), pro- (pass through: projít), na- (climb: najít — but najít = 'to find'), v- (enter: vejít). Return: vrátit se.
- **Examples:**
  - *Přišel domů v pět hodin.* — "He arrived home at five o'clock."
  - *Odšli z kina po filmu.* — "They left the cinema after the film."

### GR-262 | Aspect pairs — systematic prefix patterns
- **Pattern:** Imperfective (process) ↔ Perfective (result) — paired by prefixes: u-, na-, s-/z-, vy-, do-, při-, od-
- **Explanation:** Perfective formed by adding prefixes: u- (dělat→udělat), na- (psát→napsat), pře- (číst→přečíst), s-/z- (tvořit→stvořit). Imperfective answers 'what were you doing?', perfective answers 'what did you do?'
- **Examples:**
  - *dělat (impf) → udělat (pf)* — "to do — process → completed"
  - *psát (impf) → napsat (pf)* — "to write — process → completed"

### GR-263 | Direction adverbs — kam, odkud, tudy, tam, sem, ven
- **Pattern:** kam (where to), odkud (where from), tudy (this way), tam (there), sem (here/to here), ven (outside/out)
- **Explanation:** Czech direction adverbs: kam (destination), odkud (origin), tudy (this way), tam (there), sem (here), ven (outside), dovnitř (inside), nahoru (upward), dolů (downward). These pair with motion verbs.
- **Examples:**
  - *Kam jdeš? — Jdu do školy.* — "Where are you going? — I'm going to school."
  - *Odkud přicházíš?* — "Where are you coming from?"

### GR-264 | Aspect in context — when to use imperfective vs perfective
- **Pattern:** Imperfective for ongoing/repeated/habitual; perfective for single completed actions with results
- **Explanation:** IMPERFECTIVE for: process (psal jsem celý večer), habitual (každý den čtu), simultaneous (když jsem vařil...), asking 'what were you doing?'. PERFECTIVE for: completed action (napsal jsem e-mail), single events (koupil jsem auto), sequential actions, asking 'what did you do?'
- **Examples:**
  - *Psal jsem dopis, když telefonovala.* — "I was writing a letter when she called. (imperfective)"
  - *Napsal jsem dopis a odeslal ho.* — "I wrote a letter and sent it. (perfective)"

## Unit 30 — A1 Summary

### GR-290 | A1 grammar summary — case, tense, and agreement overview
- **Pattern:** Consolidation: nominative + accusative, present tense, gender agreement, pronouns, basic word order
- **Explanation:** (1) CASES: nominative (subject) and accusative (direct object: fem -a→-u, masc animate changes, masc inanimate/neuter unchanged). (2) TENSES: present only — být, mít, regular verbs (-á, -í, -e). (3) GENDER: three genders with adjective endings (-ý, -á, -é). (4) PRONOUNS: personal and possessive. (5) WORD ORDER: SVO default, verb in 2nd position for clitics. (6) NEGATION: ne- prefix. (7) QUESTIONS: intonation or question words.
- **Examples:**
  - *Jsem student a bydlím v Praze.* — "I am a student and I live in Prague."
  - *Mám novou knihu.* — "I have a new book. (accusative: nová→novou, kniha→knihu)"

## Unit 31 — A2 Summary

### GR-291 | A2 grammar summary — complete case system, aspect, tenses, complex sentences
- **Pattern:** Consolidation: all 6 cases, present/past/future/conditional, aspect recognition, adjective comparison, complex sentences
- **Explanation:** (1) CASES: all 6 — nominative (subject), genitive (possession/quantity), dative (indirect object), accusative (direct object), locative (v/na/o/po), instrumental (means/accompaniment). (2) TENSES: present, past (-l + jsem), future (budu + inf or perfective present), conditional (bych + -l). (3) ASPECT: imperfective (process) vs perfective (completion) recognition. (4) ADJECTIVES: comparison (-ejší/-ější, irregular: lepší/větší), superlative (nej-). (5) MODALS: muset/moct/smět/chtít. (6) CLAUSES: compound (a, ale, nebo), complex (že, protože, když, aby). (7) CLITICS: 2nd position (jsem, se, si, by). (8) MOTION: jít/jet, chodit/jezdit, prefixed forms.
- **Examples:**
  - *Kdybych měl čas, šel bych do kina.* — "If I had time, I would go to the cinema."
  - *Včera jsem koupil knihu, kterou mi doporučil učitel.* — "Yesterday I bought a book that the teacher recommended to me."
